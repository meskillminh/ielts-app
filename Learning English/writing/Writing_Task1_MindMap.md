## 📗 LESSON 1 — Building Blocks

### 1A · QUANTIFIERS — Who is the subject?

```
SUBJECT TYPE
│
├── COUNTABLE NOUNS
│       └── the number of + [noun]
│               ├── the number of students
│               ├── the number of cases
│               └── the number of tourists
│
├── UNCOUNTABLE NOUNS
│       └── the amount of + [noun]
│               ├── the amount of water
│               ├── the amount of energy
│               └── the amount of money
│
├── PERCENTAGE / PROPORTION
│       └── the percentage / proportion of + [noun]
│               ├── the percentage of households
│               └── the proportion of people who…
│
├── FIXED PHRASES (no quantifier needed)
│       ├── the unemployment rate
│       ├── the birth rate
│       ├── the death rate
│       ├── the price of [something]      ← NOT "the amount of price"
│       ├── the sales of [something]      ← NOT "the number of sales"
│       └── the income of [something]    ← NOT "the amount of income"
│
└── PRONOUN SUBSTITUTES (to avoid repetition)
        ├── this figure        ← when subject STAYS THE SAME
        └── the figure for X  ← when subject CHANGES to X
```

### 1B · TREND VERBS & NOUNS — What happened?

```
TREND DIRECTION
│
├── ⬆ INCREASE
│       │   Verb form          Noun form
│       ├── rise            →  a rise
│       ├── increase        →  an increase        ← most common
│       ├── grow            →  a growth           ← NOT for money
│       ├── climb           →  a climb
│       ├── go up           →  (no noun)
│       └── (general)       →  an upward trend
│
├── ⬇ DECREASE
│       │   Verb form          Noun form
│       ├── decrease        →  a decrease
│       ├── fall            →  a fall
│       ├── drop            →  a drop
│       ├── decline         →  a decline
│       ├── reduce          →  a reduction
│       ├── go down         →  (no noun)
│       └── (general)       →  a downward trend
│
├── ➡ STABLE
│       ├── remain unchanged at [number]
│       ├── remain constant / remain stable / remain steady
│       ├── stabilize                              ← after fluctuation
│       └── Noun: a stability                     ← "unchanged" has NO noun form
│
├── 〰 FLUCTUATE
│       ├── fluctuate between A and B              ← use only when up & down ≥ 3 times
│       └── Noun: a fluctuation
│
├── ▲ PEAK
│       ├── peak at [number]
│       ├── reach its peak of [number]
│       └── reach its highest point of [number]
│
└── ▼ BOTTOM
        └── hit its lowest point of [number]
```

### 1C · DEGREE — How much did it change?

```
LEVEL OF CHANGE
│
├── 🔴 VERY STRONG
│       ├── dramatic   / dramatically
│       ├── rapid      / rapidly          ← short time frame
│       └── sharp      / sharply          ← sudden & strong
│
├── 🟠 STRONG
│       ├── significant   / significantly  ← most versatile
│       ├── considerable  / considerably
│       ├── substantial   / substantially
│       └── remarkable    / remarkably
│
├── 🟡 MEDIUM
│       ├── gradual   / gradually         ← slow & steady
│       ├── moderate  / moderately
│       └── steady    / steadily          ← consistent pace
│
└── 🟢 SMALL
        ├── slight    / slightly
        ├── marginal  / marginally
        └── minimal   / minimally         ← almost no change
```

> 💡 **Band 7 rule:** Never use the same degree word twice in one paragraph. Rotate: *dramatically → sharp → gradual → slight*

### 1D · PREPOSITIONS — Numbers & Time

```
PREPOSITIONS
│
├── FOR NUMBERS
│       ├── rose TO 50%           ← final value (where it ended up)
│       ├── rose BY 20%           ← amount of change (how much it moved)
│       ├── from A to B           ← start point and end point
│       ├── remain unchanged AT   ← the level it stayed at
│       ├── start / stand AT      ← the level at a given moment
│       └── fluctuate BETWEEN A AND B
│
└── FOR TIME
        ├── in [year / month]
        ├── at the beginning / end of the period
        ├── over the period (from X to Y)      ← the full duration
        ├── throughout the period               ← all the way through
        ├── during most of the period
        ├── in the first / second half of the period
        ├── from [year] to [year]
        ├── between [year] and [year]
        └── over the first / last + N + years
```

> ⚠️ **KEY DIFFERENCE:**
> - *"rose **to** 50%"* → 50% is where it ended up
> - *"rose **by** 20%"* → it moved 20 percentage points (e.g. from 30% to 50%)

## 📘 LESSON 2 — 3 Sentence Structures

```
SAME DATA → 3 DIFFERENT WAYS TO WRITE IT

Example: tourist arrivals rose from 5M to 9M in 2015

┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ STRUCTURE 1   S + V + Adv + number + time                           │
│  "The number of tourists increased significantly to 9M in 2015."    │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ STRUCTURE 2   There was + a/an + adj + noun + in + S + time         │
│  "There was a significant increase to 9M in tourist arrivals        │
│   in 2015."                                                         │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ STRUCTURE 3   S + [verb] + a/an + adj + noun + time                 │
│  "Tourist arrivals recorded a significant increase to 9M in 2015."  │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### Verbs for Structure 3

| Verb | Tone | Best used for |
|---|---|---|
| **experienced** | neutral, academic | any change — safest choice |
| **witnessed** | vivid, descriptive | dramatic or surprising changes |
| **recorded** | data-driven | statistics, figures, reports |
| **saw** | natural, semi-formal | general academic writing |

### Noun Forms (for Structure 2 & 3)

| Direction | Nouns |
|---|---|
| ⬆ Increase | an increase / a rise / a growth / a climb |
| ⬇ Decrease | a decrease / a fall / a drop / a decline / a reduction |
| ➡ Stable | a stability *(note: "unchanged" has NO noun form)* |
| 〰 Fluctuate | a fluctuation |
| General | an upward trend / a downward trend |

> 💡 **Strategy:** Use Structure 1 for your opening sentence, then alternate with Structure 2 or 3. Examiners reward **grammatical variety**.

## 📙 LESSON 3 — Connectors for Full Paragraphs

### 3A · Connectors WITHIN a Sentence

```
IN-SENTENCE CONNECTORS
│
├── ⏱ TIME (joining two stages of the same subject)
│       │
│       ├── After V-ing, Clause 2
│       │       "After remaining stable until 2005, the figure rose sharply."
│       │
│       ├── Clause 1 before V-ing
│       │       "The figure remained stable until 2005 before rising sharply."
│       │
│       ├── Clause 1, and then Clause 2    ← middle of sentence only
│       │       "It remained stable until 2005, and then rose sharply."
│       │
│       └── Clause 1, which then + V       ← relative clause, academic
│               "It remained stable until 2005, which then rose sharply."
│
└── ↔ CONTRAST (comparing two different things)
        │
        ├── While / Whereas Clause 1, Clause 2
        │       "While car use rose, bus ridership declined."
        │
        ├── Clause 1, but Clause 2         ← middle of sentence only
        │       "Car use rose, but bus ridership declined."
        │
        └── Despite V-ing / Noun, Clause 2
                "Despite rising demand overall, bus ridership declined."
```

---

### 3B · Connectors BETWEEN Sentences

```
BETWEEN-SENTENCE CONNECTORS
│
├── ⏱ TIME (same or different subject, continuing the story)
│       ├── Then, …
│       ├── After that, …
│       └── Following that, …        ← most formal / academic
│
├── ↔ CONTRAST
│       ├── However, …               ← SAME subject continues
│       └── In contrast / By contrast, …  ← DIFFERENT subject starts
│
├── ≈ SIMILARITY (different subject, same direction)
│       ├── Similarly, …
│       └── Likewise, …
│
└── ~ NEUTRAL (different subject, no clear relation)
        └── Meanwhile, …
```

### Quick-Decision Guide

```
Q: Am I talking about THE SAME subject?
│
├── YES → continuing the same trend?
│           ├── YES → Then / After that / Following that
│           └── NO  → However
│
└── NO → switching to a new subject?
            ├── Same direction? → Similarly / Likewise
            ├── Opposite direction? → By contrast / In contrast
            └── No clear relation? → Meanwhile
```

### 3C · Approximation Words

```
APPROXIMATION (when data point is between gridlines)
│
├── GENERAL "around"
│       ├── about        ← most common, informal-neutral
│       ├── around       ← same as about
│       └── approximately ← most formal
│
├── "NOT QUITE THERE"
│       ├── nearly       ← approaching a round number
│       ├── almost       ← just below a round number
│       └── roughly      ← casual estimate
│
└── "JUST ABOVE / BELOW"
        ├── just over    ← slightly above a round number   e.g. "just over 50%"
        └── just under   ← slightly below a round number  e.g. "just under 50%"
```

## ⚡ QUICK REFERENCE — Common Mistakes

| ❌ Wrong | ✅ Right | Rule |
|---|---|---|
| The data **show** | The data **shows** | "data" = singular in IELTS |
| The number of students **were** | The number of students **was** | "the number" = singular subject |
| An increase **of** the number | An increase **in** the number | increase **in**, not "of" |
| Decreased from 2010 **until** 2020 | Decreased from 2010 **to** 2020 | use "to" for time ranges |
| Rose **by** 50% *(when 50% = final value)* | Rose **to** 50% | TO = final value, BY = amount of change |
| The sales **in** CDs | The sales **of** CDs | sales **of**, not "in" |
| Copy the task question word-for-word | Paraphrase the introduction | Never copy — always rephrase |

## 📝 FULL ESSAY STRUCTURE (Band 7)

```
PARAGRAPH 1 — Introduction (1–2 sentences)
  └── Paraphrase the task question
        • "The graph shows…" → "The line graph illustrates…"
        • "changes in…"     → "how X changed / trends in…"
        • "from X to Y"     → "over a N-year period"

PARAGRAPH 2 — Overview (2 sentences, NO numbers)
  └── Summarise the 2 biggest trends only
        • "Overall, it is clear that…"
        • "What stands out most is that…"
        • "Overall, [A] experienced the most dramatic change, while [B] remained stable."

PARAGRAPH 3 — Body 1 (4–5 sentences)
  └── Biggest / most important trend with specific data
        • Use "In [year], [subject] stood at [X], which then…"
        • Mix Structures 1, 2, and 3
        • Use degree adverbs to describe size of change

PARAGRAPH 4 — Body 2 (3–4 sentences)
  └── Supporting trends, comparisons, or exceptions
        • Start with "Turning to…" or "In terms of…"
        • Use "It is worth noting that…" for an interesting detail
        • Compare two subjects using "while / by contrast / similarly"

TARGET: 170–200 words total
```
